\section{Batched Proof Tools}\label{sec:tools}

This section states the two batching tools used throughout the protocol. The
first tool combines heterogeneous linear identities that initially live on
different polynomial domains. The second combines many same-form nonlinear
checks after their witness bundles have been aligned to a common shape.

\subsection{Domain-Lifted Batch}

\begin{theorem}[Domain-lifted batch]
\label{thm:domain-lifted-batch}
Let
\[
1 \leq n_1 < n_2 < \cdots < n_m = N
\]
be powers of two. For each $i \in [m]$, let $F_i(X)$ be a virtual polynomial
and suppose the target claim is
\[
F_i(X)=Q_i(X)\nu_{n_i}(X)
\]
for some polynomial $Q_i(X)$. Define the lifted residual
\[
\widehat{F}_i(X):=
F_i(X)\frac{\nu_N(X)}{\nu_{n_i}(X)}.
\]
Then:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $F_i(X)=Q_i(X)\nu_{n_i}(X)$ holds if and only if
  $\widehat{F}_i(X)$ is divisible by $\nu_N(X)$.
  \item For a verifier challenge $\eta$, all $m$ claims can be combined into
  the single identity
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:domain-lifted-batch}
  \sum_{i=1}^{m}\eta^{i-1}\widehat{F}_i(X)=Q(X)\nu_N(X).
  \end{equation}
  \item If at least one original claim is false, then
  \eqref{eq:domain-lifted-batch} holds for at most $m-1$ values of $\eta$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The first item is immediate from
\[
F_i(X)=Q_i(X)\nu_{n_i}(X)
\iff
F_i(X)\frac{\nu_N(X)}{\nu_{n_i}(X)} = Q_i(X)\nu_N(X).
\]
For soundness, once the lifted residuals are reduced modulo $\nu_N(X)$, the
left-hand side of \eqref{eq:domain-lifted-batch} becomes a polynomial in $\eta$
of degree at most $m-1$. If at least one original claim is false, this
polynomial is nonzero, so a uniformly random $\eta$ causes accidental
acceptance with probability at most $(m-1)/|\mathbb{F}|$.
\end{proof}

Theorem~\ref{thm:domain-lifted-batch} is the protocol's main tool for batching
heterogeneous linear identities. Later we apply it to routing equations,
padding-induced tensor equalities, and other zero-check-style relations whose
natural domains are not identical.

\subsection{Interleaved Batch}

The interleaving theorem applies to same-form nonlinear checks, but first we
need one alignment lemma.

\begin{lemma}[Row replication]
\label{lem:row-replication}
Let $\mathbf{v}\in\mathbb{F}^m$ and let $v(X)$ be its encoding over
$\mathbb{H}_m$. Let $\mathbf{V}\in\mathbb{F}^{r\times m}$ be the matrix whose
every row equals $\mathbf{v}$. Then the encoding polynomial of $\mathbf{V}$ over
$\mathbb{H}_{rm}$ is
\[
V(X)=v(X^r).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For every $i\in[r]$ and $j\in[m]$,
\[
V(\omega_{rm}^{im+j})
= \mathbf{V}[i][j]
= \mathbf{v}[j]
= v(\omega_m^j)
= v((\omega_{rm}^{im+j})^r),
\]
where the last equality uses $\omega_{rm}^r=\omega_m$. Since both sides have
degree less than $rm$ and agree on $\mathbb{H}_{rm}$, they are identical.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Interleaving]
Let $\mathbf{a}^{(0)},\ldots,\mathbf{a}^{(k-1)}\in\mathbb{F}^n$. Their
interleaving is the vector
\[
\operatorname{Int}\!\left(
\mathbf{a}^{(0)},\ldots,\mathbf{a}^{(k-1)}
\right)\in\mathbb{F}^{kn}
\]
defined by
\[
\operatorname{Int}\!\left(
\mathbf{a}^{(0)},\ldots,\mathbf{a}^{(k-1)}
\right)[ik+t]
:=
\mathbf{a}^{(t)}[i]
\qquad
\forall i\in[n],\ t\in[k].
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Interleaving batch]
\label{thm:interleaving-batch}
For each $\ell\in[m]$, let
\[
\mathcal{U}_\ell=
\bigl(
\mathbf{u}^{(1)}_\ell,\ldots,\mathbf{u}^{(s)}_\ell
\bigr)
\]
be a bundle of vectors over a domain of size $n_\ell$, and suppose all bundles
must satisfy the same pointwise polynomial relation
\[
\Psi\!\left(
\mathbf{u}^{(1)}_\ell[x],\ldots,\mathbf{u}^{(s)}_\ell[x]
\right)=0
\qquad
\forall x\in[n_\ell].
\]
Assume that each bundle is first aligned to a common domain size $N$ by a
shape-preserving transformation such as row replication. Let
\[
\widetilde{\mathcal{U}}_\ell=
\bigl(
\widetilde{\mathbf{u}}^{(1)}_\ell,\ldots,
\widetilde{\mathbf{u}}^{(s)}_\ell
\bigr)
\in (\mathbb{F}^N)^s
\]
denote the aligned bundle, and define the interleaved bundle
\[
\operatorname{Int}(\widetilde{\mathcal{U}}_1,\ldots,\widetilde{\mathcal{U}}_m)
:=
\Bigl(
\operatorname{Int}(\widetilde{\mathbf{u}}^{(1)}_1,\ldots,\widetilde{\mathbf{u}}^{(1)}_m),
\ldots,
\operatorname{Int}(\widetilde{\mathbf{u}}^{(s)}_1,\ldots,\widetilde{\mathbf{u}}^{(s)}_m)
\Bigr).
\]
Then all original pointwise claims hold if and only if the interleaved bundle
satisfies
\[
\Psi\!\left(
\widehat{\mathbf{u}}^{(1)}[z],\ldots,\widehat{\mathbf{u}}^{(s)}[z]
\right)=0
\qquad
\forall z\in[mN],
\]
where
\[
(\widehat{\mathbf{u}}^{(1)},\ldots,\widehat{\mathbf{u}}^{(s)})
:=
\operatorname{Int}(\widetilde{\mathcal{U}}_1,\ldots,\widetilde{\mathcal{U}}_m).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For each aligned position $x\in[N]$ and each bundle index $\ell\in[m]$, the
interleaved coordinate $z=xm+\ell-1$ stores exactly the tuple
\[
\bigl(
\widetilde{\mathbf{u}}^{(1)}_\ell[x],\ldots,
\widetilde{\mathbf{u}}^{(s)}_\ell[x]
\bigr).
\]
Hence the interleaved pointwise claim is just a re-indexing of the $mN$ aligned
pointwise claims. The equivalence follows immediately.
\end{proof}

Theorem~\ref{thm:interleaving-batch} is useful when the protocol produces many
small nonlinear checks of the same form. Examples include several lookup-style
table-difference checks and several same-form rounding relations of the form
$n=qd+r$ with bounded remainder. Rather than assign a separate batch to each
local check, the prover aligns the bundles and proves one larger pointwise
relation on the interleaved object.
