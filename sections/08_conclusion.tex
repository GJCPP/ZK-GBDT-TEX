\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper formulates a proof architecture for quantized GBDT in which the
central training difficulty, namely optimal split selection, is handled through
a dedicated primitive layer rather than through a monolithic generic circuit.
The key ingredients are a common batched zero-check template, domain-lifting
batching for heterogeneous subgroup sizes, replication and interleaving
batching for reshaping committed objects, and aggregation/histogram relations
that expose split search as tensor algebra. This organization is intended to fit the
style of a security-systems paper: the core protocol is presented as a small
catalog of relations, the imported and new components are separated cleanly,
and the tree-specific training logic remains explicit throughout the proof.
